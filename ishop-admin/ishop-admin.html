AI Insights
Revenue forecasts, regional anomalies, and restocking recommendations surfaced automatically — each with context and a clear next action. Replacing a weekly habit of digging through reports to spot what mattered.
Orders
A scannable, filterable order flow — from list to detail in one place. Advanced filters surface exactly what ops teams need, without manual export or tab-switching.
Products
A flexible catalogue with clear status tags and inline editing. Adding new products was redesigned to reduce input friction and support faster daily operations.
Inventory
Real-time stock visibility with proactive low-stock alerts. The restock workflow was redesigned from 7 steps across 2 systems down to 3 steps in one place.
Stores
All locations in one view — open hours, revenue, pickup orders, and low-stock alerts per store. No more manual aggregation across separate reports.
Customers
29,827 customer records unified with full purchase history and growth trends — replacing disconnected member data spread across separate systems.
Impact
✳ Unifying fragmented tools into one platform reduced daily tool-switching overhead by 75% — proving that operational efficiency gains come from structure, not just features.
✳ Surfacing data through trends and alerts, rather than raw numbers, gave the ops team faster visibility into revenue and stock — contributing to +18.6% revenue growth in the first month.
✳ Redesigning the restock workflow from 7 steps to 3 showed that the biggest UX wins often come from removing steps, not adding them.
